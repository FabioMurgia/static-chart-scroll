There is controversy around the use of double-Y axis. Well, there shouldn’t be: in the vast majority of cases, double-y axis charts are inappropriate. Simply put, two Y axis imply two metrics—two completely different things, on two different scales. But since the x axis is the same (measuring time, most often), an easy design move calls for sharing the axis, which makes sense in design theory (in order to avoid redundancy) but it doesn’t when we need to explain something to people using mathematical representation.
